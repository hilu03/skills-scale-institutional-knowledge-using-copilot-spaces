# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They ensure the team follows agreed processes and continuously improves their workflow.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate agile ceremonies (standups, sprint planning, retrospectives, reviews)
- Remove impediments and blockers for the team
- Coach team members on agile practices and self-organization
- Collaborate with PM and PdM to ensure backlog is actionable and well-groomed
- Track team velocity and help optimize delivery flow
- Shield the team from external disruptions

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and morale
- Foster continuous improvement culture
- Ensure smooth sprint execution with minimal blockers
- Maintain sustainable team pace

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups facilitation
- Sprint planning and retrospective sessions
- Blocker escalations to PM and stakeholders
- Team health metrics and velocity reports

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on timeline risks, resource constraints, and cross-team dependencies
- **Product Manager**: Ensures backlog items are ready and prioritized for sprint planning
- **Developers**: Removes blockers, facilitates collaboration, coaches on agile practices
- **Release Manager**: Aligns on sprint commitments and release readiness
- **UX Designer**: Coordinates on design readiness before sprint starts

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate release activities, schedules, and communications. They ensure all pre-release requirements are met and orchestrate safe, predictable deployments.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate release activities and schedules across teams
- Ensure all pre-release checklists are completed
- Communicate release status to stakeholders
- Work with QA and developers on go/no-go decisions
- Manage deployment windows and rollback procedures
- Track release metrics and post-deployment health

### Goals
- Deliver zero-downtime, predictable releases
- Minimize production incidents and deployment failures
- Maintain clear release communication to all stakeholders
- Continuously improve deployment automation

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews
- Go/no-go decision meetings
- Deployment status updates
- Post-release reports and metrics

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Aligns release schedule with project milestones
- **Developers**: Coordinates code freeze, merge readiness, and deployment steps
- **Product Manager**: Validates feature completeness and success metrics tracking
- **Support Lead**: Ensures support readiness and handoff of release notes
- **Scrum Master**: Coordinates sprint deliverables for release inclusion

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers define and validate user experience standards, creating intuitive interfaces that meet user needs. They advocate for usability, accessibility, and design consistency.

### Responsibilities
- Define user experience standards and design systems
- Deliver wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs for new features
- Collaborate closely with PdM and developers on feature design
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design
- Maintain design documentation and component libraries

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve satisfaction metrics
- Ensure design consistency across the product
- Balance user needs with technical constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings presentations
- Prototype walkthroughs with PdM and developers
- Design system documentation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on feature definition and validates user needs
- **Developers**: Works closely during implementation to ensure design fidelity
- **Scrum Master**: Ensures design work is completed before sprint commitments
- **Data Analyst**: Reviews usage metrics to inform design decisions
- **Project Manager**: Aligns on design timeline and resource needs

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts provide metrics and evidence for decision-making, enabling data-informed product and project choices. They measure success and help teams understand impact.

### Responsibilities
- Provide metrics and analytics for decision-making
- Set up tracking, dashboards, and reporting systems
- Help define and measure success criteria
- Analyze user behavior and feature adoption
- Report results and insights to stakeholders
- Identify trends and opportunities through data

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making across teams
- Provide timely, accurate insights
- Measure and report on success metrics
- Identify opportunities for optimization

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics reviews
- Dashboard walkthroughs
- Analysis reports and recommendations
- Success metrics presentations

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Defines success metrics and validates hypotheses with data
- **Project Manager**: Provides project health metrics and risk indicators
- **Developers**: Collaborates on instrumentation and tracking implementation
- **UX Designer**: Shares usage data to inform design improvements
- **Support Lead**: Analyzes support trends and user pain points

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Support Leads own the post-release feedback loop with users, triaging issues and coordinating fixes. They ensure user voices inform product improvements and maintain knowledge bases.

### Responsibilities
- Own post-release feedback loop with users
- Triage support issues and coordinate fixes with development team
- Share user insights with product and delivery teams
- Maintain and update knowledge base and support documentation
- Track support metrics (ticket volume, resolution time, satisfaction)
- Escalate critical issues and identify product gaps

### Goals
- Deliver excellent user support experience
- Reduce repeat issues and time-to-resolution
- Ensure user feedback informs product roadmap
- Maintain comprehensive support resources

### Typical Communication
- Daily support triage and escalations
- Weekly feedback summaries to product and engineering
- Knowledge base updates and documentation
- Critical issue escalations

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Shares user feedback and pain points to inform roadmap
- **Developers**: Escalates bugs and coordinates on fixes
- **Release Manager**: Receives release notes and prepares support readiness
- **Data Analyst**: Collaborates on support metrics and trend analysis
- **Project Manager**: Alerts to user-impacting issues and risks
